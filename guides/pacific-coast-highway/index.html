Pacific Coast Highway: San Francisco to Los Angeles

~500 miles via Highway 1 · 2–3 days recommended · America's most scenic coastal drive

The Pacific Coast Highway between San Francisco and Los Angeles is regularly ranked among the most beautiful drives on earth — roughly 500 miles of cliff-hugging road past Monterey, Big Sur, Hearst Castle, and the Santa Barbara coast. The freeway route (I-5, about 6 hours) will get you there, but Highway 1 is the reason you came.

Suggested 2-3 Day Itinerary

Day 1: San Francisco to Monterey/Carmel (~120 mi)

Leave the city on Highway 1 through Pacifica and Half Moon Bay. Stop at Pigeon Point Lighthouse and the elephant seals at Año Nuevo before reaching Santa Cruz. In Monterey, the aquarium is world-class; Carmel-by-the-Sea, ten minutes on, is the prettier overnight stop.

Day 2: The Big Sur Coast to San Luis Obispo (~140 mi)

This is the day the postcards are made of: Bixby Creek Bridge, Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park, McWay Falls, and endless turnouts. Traffic moves at 30-40 mph and cell coverage disappears — fill the tank in Monterey and download offline maps. Stop at Hearst Castle in San Simeon and watch the elephant seal colony at Piedras Blancas before overnighting in Cambria, Morro Bay, or San Luis Obispo.

Day 3: San Luis Obispo to Los Angeles (~190 mi)

The road mellows through Pismo Beach and the Santa Ynez wine country. Santa Barbara makes the perfect long lunch — the Old Mission and State Street are both worth an hour — before the final 95-mile run into Los Angeles. Time it to avoid arriving in LA between 4 and 7 PM.

When to Go

September and October are the sweet spot: the summer fog ("June Gloom") has burned off, crowds are thinner, and the water is at its warmest. Winter brings dramatic surf and whale migrations but also the highest landslide risk in Big Sur. Summer weekends mean heavy traffic and booked-out lodging — reserve Big Sur and Carmel rooms weeks ahead.

Costs and Practicalities

  • Gas: ~$60-70 for the coastal route at California prices. Fill up before Big Sur — the one station there charges some of the highest prices in the country.
  • Lodging: Big Sur and Carmel run $250+; Santa Cruz, Salinas, and San Luis Obispo are the value stops.
  • No tolls on Highway 1 itself; the Golden Gate Bridge toll applies only if you approach from the north.
Drive north to south. You'll hug the ocean side of the road, and every viewpoint pull-off is a simple right turn instead of a left across traffic.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to drive the Pacific Coast Highway?

San Francisco to Los Angeles on Highway 1 is about 500 miles, but plan 10-12 hours of actual driving — the road is slow, winding, and full of stops you'll want to make. Two days with a night in Monterey or San Luis Obispo is the practical minimum.

Is Highway 1 through Big Sur open?

Big Sur is periodically closed by landslides, most often in winter and spring. Check Caltrans (dot.ca.gov) before you go; when closed, US-101 provides an inland bypass between Monterey and San Luis Obispo.

Which direction is better for driving the PCH?

North to south. You'll be in the lane closest to the ocean, with easier pull-offs at the viewpoints and no oncoming traffic between you and the photos.
