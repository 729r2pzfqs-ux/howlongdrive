Florida Road Trip: Beaches, Theme Parks, and the Keys

3 classic routes · Year-round destination · Overseas Highway, theme parks & Gulf beaches

Florida packs three completely different road trips into one state: the tropical island-hop down the Overseas Highway to Key West, the theme-park corridor around Orlando, and the white-sand Gulf Coast. Distances are short by American standards, roads are flat and fast, and every route ends at water.

Miami to Key West: The Overseas Highway

The 165-mile drive down US-1 is the best road trip in the eastern US: 42 bridges strung across the Keys, including the Seven Mile Bridge at Marathon. Stop at Key Largo's John Pennekamp Coral Reef State Park (glass-bottom boat tours), Islamorada's sandbar restaurants, and Bahia Honda State Park — the best beach in the Keys. Leave Miami before 9 AM; the two-lane stretches crawl by afternoon, especially on weekends.

Miami to Orlando

The 235-mile run up Florida's Turnpike takes about 3.5 hours. Break it at Palm Beach or Lake Okeechobee. Orlando itself is a hub-and-spoke base: Disney World, Universal, and Kennedy Space Center (an hour east at Cape Canaveral) all make easy day trips.

Orlando to Tampa and the Gulf

85 miles on I-4 — Florida's busiest highway, so avoid rush hours — puts you on the Gulf Coast. St. Pete Beach, Clearwater Beach, and (two hours south) Sarasota's Siesta Key have the powder-white sand the postcards promise, plus sunsets over water.

When to Go

December-April is the dry season: 75-85°F, low humidity, peak prices. May and November are the value windows. June-October is hot, humid, and thundery, with hurricane risk peaking August-October — fine for a deal-hunting trip if you stay flexible and insured.

Practical Notes

  • Tolls: The Turnpike and Orlando expressways are cashless — use SunPass or your rental's transponder program.
  • Gas: Cheapest along I-95 and the Turnpike service plazas; the Keys run 30-50¢/gallon higher, so fill up in Florida City.
  • Summer storms: Afternoon thunderstorms are near-daily June-September. They pass in 30-60 minutes; pull over rather than drive through zero-visibility rain.
Key West parking is scarce and paid. Park once at your hotel and explore the old town on foot or by bike — everything worth seeing is within a mile.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long is the drive from Miami to Key West?

About 165 miles and 3.5-4 hours via the Overseas Highway (US-1), crossing 42 bridges including the famous Seven Mile Bridge. Allow a full day with stops — traffic on the two-lane sections backs up on weekends.

What is the best time of year for a Florida road trip?

December through April brings dry, warm weather and the biggest crowds. Hurricane season officially runs June-November, with peak risk August-October — trips then are cheaper but buy travel insurance and watch forecasts.

Do you need to pay tolls driving in Florida?

Yes — Florida's Turnpike and many Orlando-area expressways are tolled, and most have gone cashless. A SunPass or a rental car's toll transponder saves fees; the drive to Key West on US-1 is toll-free.
