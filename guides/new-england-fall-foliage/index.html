New England Fall Foliage: The Ultimate Leaf-Peeping Road Trip

4–6 days · Late September–mid October · Vermont, New Hampshire, Maine & Massachusetts

For two or three weeks every autumn, northern New England turns into the most photographed landscape in America. The good news for road-trippers: the region is compact. A 4-6 day loop from Boston covers Vermont's Green Mountains, New Hampshire's White Mountains, and the Maine coast — with every mile of it scenic.

Timing Is Everything

Foliage peaks in a wave moving north to south and summit to valley:

  • Last week of September: Northern Vermont (Stowe), the White Mountains' higher elevations.
  • First week of October: Central Vermont's Route 100, the Kancamagus Highway — the classic peak window.
  • Mid October: Southern Vermont, the Berkshires, southern New Hampshire.
  • Late October: Coastal Maine, Massachusetts, and Connecticut.

State tourism sites publish weekly foliage trackers from mid-September; check them the week before you leave and shift your loop north or south accordingly.

Days 1-2: Boston to the Green Mountains

Drive northwest toward Burlington (3.5 hours direct, but don't go direct). Detour onto VT-100, the spine of the Green Mountains — through Weston, Ludlow, Killington, and Waitsfield — arguably the best foliage road in America. Overnight in Stowe or Waterbury (Ben & Jerry's factory). Ride the Stowe gondola for color from above.

Days 3-4: The White Mountains

Cross into New Hampshire via US-2 or VT-15 to the Kancamagus Highway (NH-112) between Lincoln and Conway: 34.5 miles of national forest with hairpin overlooks and zero commercial development. Nearby: the Flume Gorge boardwalk, the Mount Washington Auto Road (weather permitting), and North Conway's outlet-town lodging.

Day 5: The Maine Coast and Home

Drop east to Portland, Maine for lobster rolls on the working waterfront and the Portland Head Light — foliage against a lighthouse is the trip's closing shot — then an easy two hours back to Boston. With a sixth day, add Cape Cod for off-season beaches.

Practical Notes

  • Book early: Peak-weekend rooms in Stowe and North Conway sell out 2-3 months ahead; Columbus Day weekend is the busiest of the year.
  • Drive on weekdays where possible — the Kancamagus and VT-100 crawl bumper-to-bumper on peak Saturdays.
  • Weather: Expect 40s-60s°F and the odd frost; mountain passes can see flurries by mid-October. Pack layers.
  • Gas and cell service both disappear on the Kancamagus — fill up in Lincoln or Conway first.
Photographer's hour: Foliage color is richest in the hour after sunrise, when low light hits the hillsides and the valley fog burns off — and the overlooks are empty.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is peak fall foliage in New England?

Color moves north to south and high to low: northern Vermont and New Hampshire's White Mountains peak in late September to early October, southern Vermont and the Berkshires in early-to-mid October, and coastal Massachusetts and Connecticut in mid-to-late October.

What is the best scenic drive for fall colors?

The Kancamagus Highway (NH-112) through the White Mountains is the consensus number one — 34.5 miles of unbroken national forest with no services, gas stations, or billboards. Vermont's Route 100 is the best multi-day corridor.

How far in advance should you book foliage-season lodging?

Two to three months for peak weekends (Columbus Day weekend sells out first). Stowe, North Conway, and Woodstock VT command $250-400/night in season; staying 30 minutes off the marquee towns halves the price.
